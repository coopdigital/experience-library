Templates for workshops and activities

We've collected a set of templates in Miro (our online collaboration tool) to allow us to run consistent and efficient workshops.

Assumption mapping

It's important to be aware of the assumptions you're making about the product or service your team is making.

This activity helps a group discuss and make clear:

  • what assumptions have we made in this product?
  • how impactful are they to the success of the product?

Go to the assumption mapping template

Avatar check-in

Use avatars to check in to workshops and meetings can help teams connect.

They give remote colleagues a digital presence and can be used to signal when someone does and does not want to input in to any activities.

Go to the avatar check-in template

Consequence scanning

Use consequence scanning to help your team to ensure the products and services you create are aligned with Co-op's values and culture.

In this workshop you'll identify the possible intended and unintended consequences of your product or service.

Go to the consequence scanning template

Design elements audit

Use this template to gather and review elements across different products to ensure that elements are consistent from one product to a next.

Go to the Design elements audit template

Contribute a template

Contribute a template using this Miro board as your starting point

When you have created your template email experiencelibrary@coop.co.uk or contact us on our support Slack channel #experience-library-support

Presentation templates

Use the presentation templates to help you present professional, consistent design work or research finings.

Co-op presentation template

Use this template to quickly format your PowerPoint presentations in a consistent and readable way.

Go to the Co-op presentation template (Co-op colleagues only).
