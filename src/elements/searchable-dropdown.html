Guidelines

Use a searchable dropdown if:

  • there's a pre-populated list of options someone can choose from
  • it’s likely that someone knows how they want to answer before seeing the list

Long lists of options can be hard for some people to use. Consider whether people may be able to answer the question more easily if you:

  • reword the question so that it has fewer options, and then use radio buttons
  • use a text input, for example for data which is very familiar to people like their date of birth

Go to the guidelines on:

  • radio buttons
  • text inputs

Order the options so that it reduces effort for people. For example, you could order alphabetically or by the most common options.

For consistency across Co-op products and services, do not change the styling of a searchable dropdown.

Size

Set the width of the dropdown so that it is appropriate for the amount of information someone needs to enter. Do not make it too wide or too narrow. Too wide and it might intimidate or confuse, too narrow and it might frustrate.

Label

A searchable dropdown must have a label that tells people what information to enter. Space form fields evenly and close to their associated label.

The label should:

  • be short
  • make it clear what information you’re asking for
  • be above the field

Hint text

You can add optional hint text to help people understand the information they need to select.

Error messages

Give error messages after the user submits the form.

Error messages should appear above the searchable dropdown list. This means they are read out by assistive technology in an order that makes sense.

Go to the guidelines on form validation.

Focus ring

The focus ring appears as a purple outline around any object, including searchable dropdowns, when someone selects it.

Do not remove the focus ring. Some people who use assistive technology need it to navigate our products and services.
