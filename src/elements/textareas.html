Guidelines

Use large textareas with caution. Some people find them intimidating or difficult to use.

Provide a textarea that is appropriate for the amount of information someone needs to enter. Do not make it too large or too small.

Do testing and research of your textareas using mobile devices. Entering information using touch devices can be time-consuming.

All attributes of the textareas are designed using values from the foundations. Do not change the styling of the textareas or the associated styles.

Go to the foundations.

Textarea field

Do not change the styling of the textarea.

We’ve designed the textareas using values from the foundations. By default, the form elements have no margin on the bottom. When using more than one form element, add a margin-bottom of 24px. This will space form fields and labels evenly down the page.

Go to the foundations.

Labels

Textareas must have a label that tells people what information to enter. The label must be short and make it clear what information you’re asking for.

Hint text

You can add optional hint text to help people understand the format of the information they need to enter. For example, you may wish to include a character limit.

Placeholder text

Placeholder text is text inside a textarea that disappears when someone selects that textarea.

Only use placeholder text if it is not essential to the person’s understanding of the information they need to enter.

Do not use placeholder text in place of labels.

Error messages

Use error messages with the validation component.

Give error messages after the user submits the form. Do not give them as someone is entering information into each textarea. This can cause stress and frustration.

Error messages should appear above the relevant textarea. This means it is read out by assistive technology in an order that makes sense.

Focus ring

The focus ring appears as a purple outline around any object, including textareas, when someone selects it.

Do not remove the focus ring. People who navigate with a keyboard cannot know where they are on the screen without it. The focus ring lets them know which textarea they have selected.
