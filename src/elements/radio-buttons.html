Guidelines

When using radio buttons you should:

  • make clear which radio button is selected
  • allow people to click or tap anywhere in the radio button or label to select that option

You should also display the options a person can choose from top to bottom, not left to right. This means that:

  • people can scan the options
  • people who have zoomed in they will not have to scroll side to side to work out which radio button they need to select

Let people tell you that the available options do not apply to them. For example, by providing a ‘None of these’ option. Some people need this to be a clearly stated option, so that they can move on to the next question or task.

All attributes of the radio buttons are designed using values from the foundations. Do not change the styling of the radio buttons or the associated styles.
